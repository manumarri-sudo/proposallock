## LAUNCH POST (Post at indiehackers.com/post/new)

**Title:**
I built ProposalLock after a client ghosted me for $4,200. Here's what I shipped and what I learned.

---

**Body:**

Eighteen months ago I lost $4,200 to a client who said the work was perfect.

He approved every revision. Responded to every message. Then stopped replying the week payment was due. By the time I accepted it wasn't coming, he had everything and I had nothing.

The problem was structural. I'd handed over the deliverables before payment cleared. Once the files were in his inbox, my leverage was zero.

I looked around for a tool that could fix this properly -- not 50% deposits (you still send the files on faith for the second half), not e-signature contracts (hard to enforce), not Dubsado or HoneyBook ($66/month for stuff I didn't need). What I wanted was simple: client pays, files unlock. That was it.

I couldn't find it, so I built it.

**What ProposalLock does:**

1. You paste a file URL (Google Drive, Dropbox, anything)
2. You set a price and write a project summary
3. You send the proposal link to your client
4. They see the breakdown and pay via Stripe
5. The files unlock automatically via webhook

No manual steps. No chasing. No "just following up on that invoice."

**What I learned building it:**

The hardest part wasn't the payment webhook. It was convincing myself clients would actually pay before seeing files. They do. Good clients don't blink. The ones who push back were going to ghost you anyway -- you've just filtered them out before you lost the work.

ProposalLock launched at $29 one-time. Not $66/month. Not a platform that takes a cut of every project. One payment, yours forever.
